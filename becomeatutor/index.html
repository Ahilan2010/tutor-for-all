Tutor-For-All is looking for committed high school students to tutor K–5 kids in math and reading. It's one of the most meaningful things you can put on a college application — and one of the most rewarding things you'll ever do.

Why Become a Tutor?

Tutoring isn't just good for students — it's one of the best investments you can make in your own future.

🎓

Strengthen Your College App

Admissions officers look for sustained, meaningful service. Teaching real students in your own community is exactly that — it's a story worth telling.

⏱️

Earn Volunteer Hours

Log community service hours toward NHS, graduation requirements, or scholarship applications. Every session counts toward your total.

🧠

Deepen Your Own Knowledge

There's no better way to truly master a subject than to teach it. You'll sharpen your own math and reading skills in the process.

🤝

Leadership Experience

Tutoring builds patience, communication, and mentorship skills — exactly the kind of leadership colleges and employers want to see.

❤️

Real Community Impact

You'll be helping a child in your own community who can't access private tutoring. That kind of direct impact is something you'll remember for life.

📄

Resume & Reference

Add Tutor-For-All to your resume and request a reference letter from our team for internships, jobs, or college applications.

Who Can Apply?

We set a high bar because our students deserve the best. If you meet these requirements, we want to hear from you.

Tutor Requirements

  • 📊
    4.0 Unweighted GPA (minimum)
    You must maintain a 4.0 GPA on the unweighted scale — all A's in your courses.
  • ➕
    90 or above in Math class
    You should have earned (or be earning) at least a 90 in your current or most recent math course.
  • 📖
    90 or above in Reading / ELA class
    You should have earned (or be earning) at least a 90 in your current or most recent English/Reading course.
  • 🏫
    Currently enrolled in high school
    Open to all high school students (grades 9–12) in the Houston area.
  • 📅
    Available at least 1–2 hours per week
    Sessions are flexible and scheduled around your availability. Commitment and consistency matter most.

Not quite there yet?

If you're close to meeting these requirements or will qualify next semester, fill out the form anyway. We'll keep your application on file and reach out when a spot opens up.

How It Works — Step by Step

From application to your first session in four simple steps.

1

Submit Your Application

Fill out the form below with your name, email, grades, availability, and the subjects you'd like to tutor. Takes less than 5 minutes.

2

We Review & Reach Out

Our team reviews your application and verifies your grades. We'll contact you within 3–5 school days to confirm your spot or ask any follow-up questions.

3

Quick Tutor Orientation

New tutors attend a short virtual orientation (video call) to go over expectations, session structure, and how to work with elementary students effectively.

4

Get Matched & Start Tutoring

We match you with a K–5 student based on grade level, subject needs, and your schedule. All sessions are held online via video call (Zoom, Google Meet, etc.).
