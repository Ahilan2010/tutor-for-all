The Problem Is Real

40%

40% of children in poverty are not prepared for primary school, compared to their higher-income peers — an educational gap that widens every year.

70%

60–70% of students in the Greater Houston area attend underfunded schools that lack the resources students need to reach their potential.

80%

80% of HISD students come from low-income families — yet most cannot afford the private tutoring that could help close the achievement gap.

Tutor-For-All was created to change this. We target the gap early — at the elementary level — before it becomes too wide to close.

How It Works

Simple, structured, and effective. No cost for families, real rewards for tutors.

✋

High School Tutors Apply

Motivated high school students with strong grades (4.0 GPA, 90+ in math & reading) sign up to tutor one-on-one sessions.

📋

Families Request Tutoring

Parents of K–5 students from low-income households submit a short form. We verify eligibility and match students with a tutor.

📚

Sessions Begin — Free

Tutors and students meet for structured math and reading sessions. Progress is tracked and families stay informed throughout.

Reaching Students Early, When It Matters Most

Research shows that students who fall behind in elementary school — especially in math and reading — rarely catch up without intervention. For families who can't afford private tutoring, that gap stays open.

Tutor-For-All was founded by students at Carnegie Vanguard High School in Houston to bridge this divide. We recruit top-performing peers to tutor younger students in their community, and we've already donated over 350 books to kids who need them.
